GDP Growth Asia and the Pacific

After slowdown, modest recovery expected

New projections of GDP in the ESCAP region show modest increases in growth in 2014, after a sharp decline in growth from the post-crisis peak in 2010.

Subregional contributions to ESCAP GDP growth rates, 2004 - 2014GDP growth rates of ESCAP subregions, 2004 - 2014

Percentage contributions of the ESCAP subregions to the overall GDP growth rate, weighted by share of regional GDP. Subregional shares sum to regional growth rate. Projections are shaded in grey. Data taken from ESCAP database. Individual GDP growth rates of ESCAP subregions, unweighted. Projections are shaded in grey. Data taken from ESCAP database.

The contributions to growth provided by large economies has decreased significantly over time, in particular with regards to East and Northeast Asia.

The improved projections are buoyed primarily by higher growth rates in South and Southwest Asia, after significant decreases in growth rates in several key regions, including East and Northeast Asia and South and Southwest Asia, hampered growth during 2011 and 2012.
